Phylogenetic Tree

With the phylogenetic tree shown above, we know that the traits in the hatch marks are only present in the organisms beyond that line. For example, bipedalism is only present in kangaroos but not on any other animal listed below its hatch mark. Additionally, a node between two organisms represents their MRCA or most recent common ancestor and all of the traits that this ancestor had are also present in its descendants.

Analysis

A. The three organisms highlighted in the monophyletic group below all have a vertebral column and limbs. This tells us that they had a common ancestor a while back from whom they got these traits from and they have underwent speciation events afterwards as specified by the nodes in the cladogram. These speciation events turn one species of an organism into two and the descendants inherit the traits which the ancestral organism had.
B. Attached below is the image of the cladogram with highlighted parts for the monophyletic groups and paraphyletic groups. The monophyletic group had their MRCA at node 4(from the bottom) and all of its descendants are highlighted which represents a clade. As for the paraphyletic group, only two descendants of the ancestor at node 2(from the bottom) are highlighted. This represents a paraphyletic group since their MRCA is included but not all of its descendants.
